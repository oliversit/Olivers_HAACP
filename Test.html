The employee will record the calibration check and any necessary corrective actions each time a thermometer is calibrated. Management will verify that employees are using and calibrating thermometers correctly through visual observation of calibration activities at least monthly. Retain this log for 2 years minimum

How to Calibrate a Thermometer

1.      Fill a container with ice (crushed ice works best) and add water. There should be more ice than water.

2.      Place the thermometer in the water so it is submerged past the indication on the stem (the indent). For infrared thermometers, point at the surface of the water.

3.      Wait at least 1 minute for the thermometer to adjust.

4.      Read the thermometer and record the temperature.

a.      If thermometer reads 32°F +/- 2°F thermometer is calibrated. If not, adjustment is necessary.
